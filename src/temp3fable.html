Wagyu 101

Cook It Right.

Wagyu isn't like other beef — its fat begins to render at a lower temperature, which makes it more forgiving and more flavorful. A few house rules from our kitchen:

1
Season simplySalt and pepper is all it needs. Let the marbling do the talking.
2
Sear hot, finish fastA ripping-hot cast iron pan and less time than you think — the rendered fat bastes the steak from within.
3
Rest before slicingGive it 5–10 minutes so the juices settle back through the cut.
Medium Rare
130–135°F
Pull at 125°F & rest

The sweet spot for Wagyu — the intramuscular fat is fully rendered, carrying flavor through every bite.

Good Questions

Before You Order

Hanging weight (HW) is the weight after initial processing — it's the standard way farms price bulk beef. You pay $7 per pound of hanging weight with processing included, and take home roughly 60% of that weight in finished, vacuum-sealed cuts. A half animal (~375 lbs HW) yields around 220 lbs of beef for your freezer.

A good rule of thumb is 1 cubic foot per 35–40 lbs of beef. A quarter fits in about 4–5 cu ft, a half in 7–9 cu ft (a standard chest freezer), and a whole in 14–16 cu ft.
